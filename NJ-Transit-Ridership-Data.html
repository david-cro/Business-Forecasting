The naive forecast simply follows the data from the previous period to forecast for the current period. For this data, this leaves us with a growing confidence interval around the July 2025 figure.

The mean forecast sets the current forecast equal to the mean of all previous periods. For this data, this means the forecast is equal to the mean so far.

The random walk forecast is based on the data from the previous period, but added with a drift variable. For this data, this leaves us with a growing confidence interval around the July 2025 figure with some drift.

The MA5 forecast is a moving average of the previous 5 periods, focusing on the most recent data. For this data, this resembles the original data but smoothed.

The MA9 forecast is a moving average of the previous 9 periods, focusing on the most recent data but over a longer time than MA5. For this data, this resembles the original data but smoothed- even more than MA5.

The Holt Winters forecast is a an exponential smoothing method that combines the forecast and actual data from the previous period, as well as seasonality. For this data, this returns a confidence interval around the July 2025 figure, but with some curve to account for seasonality.

The seasonal naive forecast is equal to the data from the same point in the previous season. For this data, this means the forecast for the upcoming months is the same as the same time last year.
